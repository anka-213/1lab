Faithful, fully faithfulüîó

Slicing preserves faithfulness and fully-faithfulness. It does not preserve fullness: Even if, by fullness, we get a map f:x‚Üíy‚ààCf : x \to y \in \ca{C}f:x‚Üíy‚ààC from a map h:F(x)‚ÜíF(y)‚ààD/F(X)h : F(x) \to F(y) \in \ca{D}/F(X)h:F(x)‚ÜíF(y)‚ààD/F(X), it does not necessarily restrict to a map in C/X\ca{C}/XC/X. Left exactnessüîó

If FFF is lex (meaning it preserves pullbacks and the terminal object), then F/XF/XF/X is lex as well. We note that it (by definition) preserves products, since products in C/X\ca{C}/XC/X are equivalently pullbacks in C/X\ca{C}/XC/X. Pullbacks are also immediately shown to be preserved, since a square in C/X\ca{C}/XC/X is a pullback iff it is a pullback in C\ca{C}C.
