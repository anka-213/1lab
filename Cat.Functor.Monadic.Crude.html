We present a refinement of the conditions laid out in Beck‚Äôs coequaliser for when an adjunction F‚ä£GF \dashv GF‚ä£G is monadic: The crude monadicity theorem. The proof we present is adapted from [Mac Lane and Moerdijk (1994), chap. IV; sect. 4], where it is applied to the setting of elementary topoi, but carried out in full generality.

Recall our setup. We have a left adjoint functor F:C‚ÜíDF : \ca{C} \to \ca{D}F:C‚ÜíD (call its right adjoint UUU), and we‚Äôre interested in characterising exactly when the comparison functor K:D‚ÜíCUFK : \ca{D} \to \ca{C}^{UF}K:D‚ÜíCUF into the Eilenberg-Moore category of the monad from the adjunction is an equivalence.   2. If, in addition, UUU preserves coequalisers for any pair which has a common right inverse, then the unit of the adjunction Œ∑\etaŒ∑ is a natural isomorphism;

  3. If, in addition, UUU is conservative, then the counit of the adjunction Œ∑\etaŒ∑ is also a natural isomorphism, and KKK is an equivalence of precategories.
