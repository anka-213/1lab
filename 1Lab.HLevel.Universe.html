Universes of n-typesüîó

A common phenomenon in higher category theory is that the collection of all nnn-categories in a given universe ‚Ñì\ell‚Ñì assembles into an (n+1)(n+1)(n+1)-category in the successor universe 1+‚Ñì1+\ell1+‚Ñì:

  • The collection of all sets (0-categories) is a (1-)-category;
  • The collection of all (1-)categories is a 2-category;
  • The collection of all 2-categories is a 3-category;

Because of the univalence axiom, the same phenomenon can be observed in homotopy type theory: The subuniverse of ‚Ñì\ell‚Ñì consisting of all nnn-types is a (n+1)(n+1)(n+1)-type in 1+‚Ñì1+\ell1+‚Ñì. That means: the universe of propositions is a set, the universe of sets is a groupoid, the universe of groupoids is a bigroupoid, and so on.
